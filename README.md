# Novo Nordisk – Revenue & Segment Trend Analysis

A data analysis project examining Novo Nordisk's public financial performance
from 2015 to early 2026, using SQL for data extraction and Python for
analysis and visualization.

## Objective

Novo Nordisk has seen exceptional revenue growth over the past decade,
largely driven by its GLP-1 product portfolio (Ozempic, Wegovy, Rybelsus).
This project explores that growth pattern: how fast is revenue growing,
which business segment is driving it, and how consistent is growth on a
quarterly basis.

## Data Source

All figures are taken from Novo Nordisk's official public disclosures:
- Annual Reports / 20-F filings (available via SEC EDGAR and Novo Nordisk's
  investor relations site)
- Historical revenue series cross-checked against MacroTrends

No figures are estimated or simulated — all values are as officially reported.

## Tools & Method

- **SQL (SQLite)**: data is loaded into an in-memory SQL database and queried
  using `SELECT`, `GROUP BY`, `CASE WHEN`, and a window function (`LAG`) to
  calculate year-over-year growth.
- **Python (pandas, matplotlib)**: query results are turned into charts —
  revenue trend, YoY growth, segment mix, and quarterly trend.
- **scikit-learn**: a simple linear regression model projects revenue for
  2026-2027 based on the historical trend.

## Key Findings

- Total annual revenue grew from ~$16.1B (2015) to ~$46.8B (2025) — roughly 3x.
- Growth accelerated sharply from 2022 onward, peaking at 34.6% YoY in 2023.
- The Diabetes & Obesity Care segment's share of total segment revenue rose
  from 84.3% (2019) to 93.6% (2024), showing increasing concentration in
  that segment.
- Quarterly revenue has trended consistently upward through Q1 2026, with no
  signs of slowdown.
- A linear regression model (R² = 0.80) projects revenue reaching roughly
  $42.7B in 2026 and $45.7B in 2027 if the historical trend continues.
